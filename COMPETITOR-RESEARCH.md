# The Mat — Competitor Research & Design Brief

_Research via Firecrawl, 2026-07-08. Goal: build a founding-member capture site that out-classes every indoor-golf site in Georgia and feels more premium than the national benchmarks._

---

## DESIGN BRIEF — Takeaways (build to these)

1. **Scarcity + exclusivity converts.** The strongest model (Future Country Club) is literally **SOLD OUT with a waitlist** and caps every tier. Impact Golf Lounge sells "become one of our **35 VIP members**." Atlanta Golf & Social states memberships are **"limited in order to preserve a premium experience."** → The Mat leans hard on: **one single bay, a finite number of founding spots, a rate that disappears.**
2. **One clear action.** The best sites have a single dominant CTA (Book / Join Waitlist). The Mat's is narrower still: **get notified for opening day + lock the founding first-month price (revealed Aug 1).** Everything points there.
3. **Premium feel = restraint + editorial typography + real atmosphere.** The client's chosen reference (Banksy event venue) wins with an **engraved small-caps serif, forest-green + cream + gold palette, alternating sections, and offset photo collages** — not neon and clutter. We match that and add cinematic dark golf-bay imagery.
4. **24/7 access is the category's headline benefit.** Back Nine and Future Country Club both lead with round-the-clock, app/QR access. The Mat: **"Open When You Are — 24/7."**
5. **Members-first community (leagues, events, guest passes)** is table stakes for the premium tier — include it as founding perks.
6. **What the weak ones get wrong (do the opposite):** Buford Indoor Golf = a bare Wix page, one paragraph, a contact form, no story, no scarcity, no atmosphere. Georgia Swing Shack = "unbeatable price" bargain framing. → The Mat never competes on cheap; it competes on **exclusivity, design, and belonging.**

## Section-order patterns (what premium sites ship)

Hero (video/atmosphere + one CTA) → value props / "why members love it" → membership tiers → community/leagues → events → locations/hours → FAQ → footer CTA. The Mat adapts this to a **pre-launch capture** flow: Hero + email → The Vision → Founding Offer (money) → What's Coming (routed stubs) → Build-in-public gallery → FAQ → final capture.

## Scarcity / exclusivity language to emulate
- "We're sold out. Join the waitlist to be notified when spots open." (FCC)
- "Memberships are limited in order to preserve a premium experience." (AGS)
- "Become one of our 35 VIP members." (Impact)
→ The Mat: **"Founding memberships are open — for now."** · "Once they're gone, this rate never comes back." · "One room. One bay. When it's yours, it's yours."

## Color / type direction (from the client's Banksy reference + his logo)
- **Forest/hunter green + warm cream/parchment + gold** metallic accent; alternating green/cream sections.
- **Engraved small-caps serif display (Cinzel/Trajan-style)** + clean grotesk body (Outfit) + tracked uppercase micro-labels.
- Cinematic dark single-bay imagery for hero/marquee; offset overlapping photo collages.

## What makes the premium ones feel premium (and the cheap ones cheap)
- **Premium:** atmosphere-first hero, disciplined palette, editorial type, scarcity, clear membership ladder, community proof, professional photography.
- **Cheap:** stock hero, generic sans, "great prices" messaging, contact form instead of a funnel, no story, no scarcity, no design point of view.
